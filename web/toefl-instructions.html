üìã Test Structure Overview

The Speaking section consists of 4 tasks that assess your ability to communicate effectively in English in academic settings. You will be evaluated on delivery, language use, and topic development.

üìö DETAILED TASK BREAKDOWN
TASK 1: Independent Speaking Task Personal Opinion

Description: You will be asked to express and defend a personal opinion about familiar topics based on your own ideas, opinions, and experiences.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing: 15 seconds preparation + 45 seconds response

What you'll do:

  • Read a question about a familiar topic
  • Prepare your response (15 seconds)
  • Record your spoken response (45 seconds)

Example topics: Education preferences, lifestyle choices, personal experiences, opinions on social issues

TASK 2: Integrated Speaking Task (Campus Situation) Read + Listen + Speak

Description: You will read a short passage about a campus-related topic, then listen to a conversation discussing the same topic, and finally speak about it.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing: 50 seconds reading + ~60 seconds listening + 30 seconds preparation + 60 seconds response

What you'll do:

  • Read a campus announcement, policy, or proposal (50 seconds)
  • Listen to students discussing their opinions about it
  • Prepare your response (30 seconds)
  • Summarize the students' opinions and reasons (60 seconds)

Focus: Accurately convey the students' attitudes and supporting reasons

TASK 3: Integrated Speaking Task (Academic Course Topic) Read + Listen + Speak

Description: You will read a short academic passage, then listen to a professor's lecture on the same topic, and explain how the lecture relates to the reading.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing: 50 seconds reading + ~60 seconds listening + 30 seconds preparation + 60 seconds response

What you'll do:

  • Read an academic passage defining a concept (50 seconds)
  • Listen to a professor explaining the concept with examples
  • Prepare your response (30 seconds)
  • Explain the concept using information from both sources (60 seconds)

Academic subjects: Psychology, Biology, Business, History, Sociology, etc.

TASK 4: Integrated Speaking Task (Academic Lecture) Listen + Speak

Description: You will listen to part of an academic lecture and then summarize the important information from the lecture.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing: ~90 seconds listening + 20 seconds preparation + 60 seconds response

What you'll do:

  • Listen to an academic lecture excerpt
  • Take notes while listening
  • Prepare your response (20 seconds)
  • Summarize the main points and supporting details (60 seconds)

Focus: Demonstrate understanding of academic content and ability to organize information clearly

üìä OFFICIAL SCORING CRITERIA

Each response is evaluated by certified ETS raters on three key dimensions:

Criterion Score Range Description
Delivery 0-4 Pronunciation, fluency, pace, and clarity of speech
Language Use 0-4 Grammar accuracy, vocabulary range, and sentence complexity
Topic Development 0-4 Content relevance, idea progression, and coherence

üéØ Score Conversion

Your raw scores (0-4 for each task) are converted to a scaled score of 0-30 for the Speaking section. This scaled score is what appears on your official TOEFL score report.

üí° Scoring Tips

  • Stay on topic and organize your ideas logically

  • Practice connecting ideas with transition words
