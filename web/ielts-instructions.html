üìã Test Overview

The IELTS Speaking test assesses your English speaking skills through a face-to-face conversation with an examiner. You will be evaluated on fluency, vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation across three distinct parts.

üìö DETAILED PART BREAKDOWN
PART 1: Introduction and Interview 4-5 minutes

Description: The examiner will introduce themselves and ask you to confirm your identity. You'll then answer general questions about familiar topics.

‚è±Ô∏è Duration: 4-5 minutes (Introduction + Q&A)

What you'll discuss:

  • Home/Accommodation: Where you live, your neighborhood, changes
  • Work/Studies: Your job, studies, daily routine, future plans
  • Familiar Topics: Hobbies, family, food, transport, technology, etc.

üí° Tips: Keep answers natural but detailed (30-45 seconds per response). Use personal examples and experiences. Show range of vocabulary and grammar.

PART 2: Long Turn (Individual Presentation) 3-4 minutes

Description: You'll receive a task card with a topic and points to cover. After 1 minute preparation, you'll speak for 1-2 minutes on the topic.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing: 1 minute preparation + 1-2 minutes speaking + follow-up questions

Task card structure:

  • Main topic with 3-4 specific points to address
  • Usually covers: describe, explain, and say how you feel/why important
  • Topics: personal experiences, people, places, objects, events

üìù Example topics: Describe a memorable journey, a person who influenced you, a skill you'd like to learn, a place you'd recommend to tourists

üí° Strategy: Use preparation time to plan structure and key vocabulary. Speak for the full time allocated. Cover all points on the card.

PART 3: Two-way Discussion 4-5 minutes

Description: The examiner will ask more complex questions related to the topic from Part 2. This tests your ability to discuss abstract ideas and give opinions.

‚è±Ô∏è Duration: 4-5 minutes of in-depth discussion

Question types:

  • Opinion questions: "What do you think about...?"
  • Comparison questions: "How has this changed over time?"
  • Hypothetical questions: "What might happen if...?"
  • Analysis questions: "Why do you think this happens?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: This part tests abstract thinking and complex language. Avoid simple yes/no answers. Develop your ideas with examples and explanations.

üìä OFFICIAL BAND SCORE CRITERIA

Your speaking is assessed equally across four criteria, each contributing 25% to your final band score:

Criterion Weight Key Assessment Points
Fluency & Coherence 25% Speaking smoothly, logical flow, appropriate linking, confidence
Lexical Resource 25% Vocabulary range, accuracy, appropriateness, natural usage
Grammatical Range & Accuracy 25% Sentence variety, complex structures, error frequency and impact
Pronunciation 25% Clear speech, stress/intonation, individual sounds, rhythm

üéØ Band Score Range

IELTS uses a 9-band scoring system:

Band 9 Expert User - Full operational command
Band 8 Very Good User - Occasional inaccuracies
Band 7 Good User - Flexible and precise language
Band 6 Competent User - Generally effective
Band 5 Modest User - Partial command
Band 4 Limited User - Basic competence
